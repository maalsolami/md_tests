From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/01_khafre_north.jpg/250px-01_khafre_north.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:01_khafre_north.jpg)

[Pyramid of Khafre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_Khafre "Pyramid of Khafre"), Egypt, built c. 2600 BC

A **pyramid** (from [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language "Ancient Greek language") [πυραμίς](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%80%CF%85%CF%81%CE%B1%CE%BC%CE%AF%CF%82#Ancient_Greek "wikt:πυραμίς") _(_puramís_)_ 'pyramid',[[1]](#cite_note-1)[[2]](#cite_note-2) from the Egyptian _pir-em-us_, the vertical height of the structure.[[3]](#cite_note-3)) is a [structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonbuilding_structure "Nonbuilding structure") whose visible surfaces are triangular in broad outline and converge toward the top, making the appearance roughly a [pyramid in the geometric sense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_\(geometry\) "Pyramid (geometry)"). The base of a pyramid can be of any [polygon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polygon "Polygon") shape, such as [triangular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triangular "Triangular") or [quadrilateral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quadrilateral "Quadrilateral"), and its surface-lines either filled or stepped.

A pyramid has the majority of its mass closer to the ground[[4]](#cite_note-4) with less mass towards the [pyramidion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramidion "Pyramidion") at the [apex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apex_\(geometry\) "Apex (geometry)"). This is due to the gradual decrease in the cross-sectional area along the vertical axis with increasing elevation. This offers a weight distribution that allowed early civilizations to create monumental structures.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Koh_Ker_temple%282007%29.jpg/250px-Koh_Ker_temple%282007%29.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Koh_Ker_temple\(2007\).jpg)

Prasat Thom temple at [Koh Ker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koh_Ker "Koh Ker"), Cambodia

Ancient [civilizations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilization "Civilization") in many parts of the world pioneered the building of pyramids. The largest pyramid by volume is the Mesoamerican [Great Pyramid of Cholula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Cholula "Great Pyramid of Cholula"), in the Mexican state of [Puebla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puebla "Puebla"). For millennia, the [largest structures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_buildings_in_the_world "List of largest buildings in the world") on Earth were pyramids—first the [Red Pyramid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Pyramid "Red Pyramid") in the [Dashur Necropolis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dashur_Necropolis "Dashur Necropolis") and then the [Great Pyramid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Giza "Great Pyramid of Giza") of [Khufu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khufu "Khufu"), both in [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt "Egypt")—the latter is the only extant example of the [Seven Wonders of the Ancient World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_Wonders_of_the_Ancient_World "Seven Wonders of the Ancient World").

## Ancient monuments

### West Asia

#### Mesopotamia

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/White_Temple_ziggurat_in_Uruk.jpg/250px-White_Temple_ziggurat_in_Uruk.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:White_Temple_ziggurat_in_Uruk.jpg)

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/The_White_Temple_%27E_at_Uruk%2C_3500-3000_BCE.jpg/250px-The_White_Temple_%27E_at_Uruk%2C_3500-3000_BCE.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:The_White_Temple_%27E_at_Uruk,_3500-3000_BCE.jpg)

Anu ziggurat and White Temple at Uruk

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Choghazanbil2.jpg/250px-Choghazanbil2.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Choghazanbil2.jpg)

[Chogha Zanbil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chogha_Zanbil "Chogha Zanbil") is an ancient Elamite complex in the Khuzestan province of [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran").

The [Mesopotamians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesopotamia "Mesopotamia") built the earliest pyramidal structures, called _[ziggurats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ziggurat "Ziggurat")_. In ancient times, these were brightly painted in [gold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold "Gold")/[bronze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze "Bronze"). They were constructed of sun-dried mud-brick, and little remains of them. Ziggurats were built by the [Sumerians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumer "Sumer"), [Babylonians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babylon "Babylon"), [Elamites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elam "Elam"), [Akkadians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akkadian_Empire "Akkadian Empire"), and [Assyrians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyria "Assyria"). Each ziggurat was part of a temple complex that included other buildings. The ziggurat's precursors were raised platforms that date from the [Ubaid period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubaid_period "Ubaid period")[[5]](#cite_note-Crawford,_page_73-5) of the fourth [millennium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millennium "Millennium") BC.

The earliest ziggurats began near the end of the [Early Dynastic Period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_Dynastic_Period_of_Sumer#Early_Dynastic_period "Early Dynastic Period of Sumer").[[6]](#cite_note-6) The original pyramidal structure, the anu ziggurat, dates to around 4000 BC. The White Temple was built on top of it circa 3500 BC.[[7]](#cite_note-7) Built in receding tiers upon a rectangular, oval, or square platform, the ziggurat was a pyramidal structure with a flat top. Sun-baked [bricks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brick "Brick") made up the core of the ziggurat with facings of fired bricks on the outside. The facings were often glazed in different colors and may have had [astrological](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astrology "Astrology") significance. Kings sometimes had their names engraved on them. The number of tiers ranged from two to seven. It is assumed that they had shrines at the top, but no archaeological evidence supports this and the only textual evidence is from [Herodotus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herodotus "Herodotus").[[8]](#cite_note-8) Access to the shrine would have been by a series of ramps on one side of the ziggurat or by a spiral ramp from base to summit.

### Africa

#### Egypt

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Giza-pyramids.JPG/250px-Giza-pyramids.JPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Giza-pyramids.JPG)

The pyramids of the [Giza necropolis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giza_pyramid_complex "Giza pyramid complex"), as seen from the air

The most famous African pyramids are in Egypt—huge structures built of bricks or stones, primarily limestone, some of which are among the world's largest constructions. They are shaped in reference to the sun's rays. Most had a smoothed white [limestone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limestone "Limestone") surface. Many of the facing stones have fallen or were removed and used for construction in [Cairo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo "Cairo").[[9]](#cite_note-9) The capstone was usually made of limestone, granite or basalt and some were plated with [electrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrum "Electrum").[[10]](#cite_note-pennstate-pyramid-10)

Ancient Egyptians built pyramids from 2700 BC until around 1700 BC. The first pyramid was erected during the [Third Dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_Dynasty "Third Dynasty") by the Pharaoh [Djoser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Djoser "Djoser") and his architect [Imhotep](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imhotep "Imhotep"). This [step pyramid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Step_pyramid "Step pyramid") consisted of six stacked [mastabas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mastaba "Mastaba").[[11]](#cite_note-11)[[12]](#cite_note-Davidovits2008-12) Early kings such as [Snefru](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sneferu "Sneferu") built pyramids, with subsequent kings adding to the number until the end of the [Middle Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Kingdom_of_Egypt "Middle Kingdom of Egypt"). The age of the pyramids reached its zenith at [Giza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giza_Necropolis "Giza Necropolis") in 2575–2150 BC.[[13]](#cite_note-13) The last king to [build royal pyramids was Ahmose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_Ahmose "Pyramid of Ahmose"),[[14]](#cite_note-14) with later kings hiding their [tombs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tomb "Tomb") in the hills, such as those in the [Valley of the Kings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valley_of_the_Kings "Valley of the Kings") in Luxor's West Bank.[[15]](#cite_note-15) In [Medinat Habu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medinet_Habu "Medinet Habu") and [Deir el-Medina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deir_el-Medina "Deir el-Medina"), smaller pyramids were built by individuals. Smaller pyramids with steeper sides were built by the [Nubians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nubians "Nubians") who ruled Egypt in the Late Period.[[16]](#cite_note-Harpur1997-16)

The [Great Pyramid of Giza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Giza "Great Pyramid of Giza") is the largest in Egypt and one of the largest in the world. At 146.6 metres (481 ft) it was the tallest structure in the world until the [Lincoln Cathedral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lincoln_Cathedral "Lincoln Cathedral") was finished in 1311 AD. Its base covers an area of around 53,000 square metres (570,000 sq ft). The Great Pyramid is the only extant one of the [Seven Wonders of the Ancient World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_Wonders_of_the_Ancient_World "Seven Wonders of the Ancient World").

Ancient Egyptian pyramids were, in most cases, placed west of the river [Nile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile "Nile") because the divine pharaoh's soul was meant to join with the sun during its descent before continuing with the sun in its eternal round.[[10]](#cite_note-pennstate-pyramid-10) As of 2008, some 135 pyramids had been discovered in Egypt,[[17]](#cite_note-17)[[18]](#cite_note-18) most located near Cairo.[[19]](#cite_note-Filer2006-19)

#### Sudan

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Nubian20pyramids.jpg/250px-Nubian20pyramids.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Nubian20pyramids.jpg)

Pyramids at [Meroe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meroe "Meroe") with [pylon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pylon_\(architecture\) "Pylon (architecture)")-like entrances

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Archaeological_Sites_of_the_Island_of_Meroe-114973.jpg/250px-Archaeological_Sites_of_the_Island_of_Meroe-114973.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Archaeological_Sites_of_the_Island_of_Meroe-114973.jpg)

Nubian pyramids at archaeological sites of the Island of Meroe

While African pyramids are commonly associated with Egypt, Sudan has 220 extant pyramids, the most in the world.[[20]](#cite_note-20) Nubian pyramids were constructed (roughly 240 of them) at three sites in [Sudan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan "Sudan") to serve as tombs for the kings and queens of [Napata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napata "Napata") and [Meroë](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mero%C3%AB "Meroë"). The pyramids of Kush, also known as [Nubian Pyramids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nubian_pyramids "Nubian pyramids"), have different characteristics than those of Egypt. The Nubian pyramids had steeper sides than the Egyptian ones. Pyramids were built in Sudan as late as 200 AD.

#### Sahel

The Tomb of Askia, in [Gao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gao "Gao"), [Mali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali "Mali"), is believed to be the burial place of [Askia Mohammad I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Askia_Mohammad_I "Askia Mohammad I"), one of the [Songhai Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Songhai_Empire "Songhai Empire")'s most prolific [emperors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperors "Emperors"). It was built at the end of the fifteenth century and is designated as a [UNESCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO") [World Heritage Site](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Heritage_Site "World Heritage Site").

[UNESCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO") describes the tomb as an example of the monumental mud-building traditions of the West African [Sahel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahel "Sahel"). The complex includes the pyramidal tomb, two [mosques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mosque "Mosque"), a [cemetery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cemetery "Cemetery") and an assembly ground. At 17 metres (56 ft) in height it is the largest pre-[colonial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonialism "Colonialism") [architectural](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Architecture "Architecture") monument in Gao. It is a notable example of the [Sudano-Sahelian architectural](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudano-Sahelian_architecture "Sudano-Sahelian architecture") style that later spread throughout the region.

#### Nigeria

One of the unique structures of [Igbo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Igbo_people "Igbo people") culture was the Nsude pyramids, in the Nigerian town of Nsude, northern [Igboland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Igboland "Igboland"). Ten pyramidal structures were built of clay/mud. The first base section was 60 ft (18 m) in circumference and 3 ft (0.9 m) in height. The next stack was 45 ft (14 m) in circumference. Circular stacks continued to the top. The structures were temples for the god [Ala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ala_\(Odinani\) "Ala (Odinani)"), who was believed to reside there. A stick was placed at the top to represent the god's residence. The structures were laid in groups of five parallel to each other. Because it was built of clay/mud like the Deffufa of Nubia, over time periodic reconstruction has been required.[[21]](#cite_note-21)

### Europe

#### Greece

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Elliniko_Piramid_-_panoramio.jpg/250px-Elliniko_Piramid_-_panoramio.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Elliniko_Piramid_-_panoramio.jpg)

[Pyramid of Hellinikon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_Hellinikon "Pyramid of Hellinikon")

[Pausanias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pausanias_\(geographer\) "Pausanias (geographer)") (2nd century AD) mentions two buildings resembling pyramids, one, 19 kilometres (12 mi) southwest of a still standing structure at Hellenikon,[[22]](#cite_note-22) a common tomb for soldiers who died in a legendary struggle for the throne of [Argos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argos,_Peloponnese "Argos, Peloponnese") and another that he was told was the tomb of Argives killed in a battle around 669/8 BC. Neither survives and no evidence indicates that they resembled Egyptian pyramids.

At least two surviving pyramid-like structures are available to study, one at [Hellenikon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_Hellinikon "Pyramid of Hellinikon") and the other at Ligourio/Ligurio, a village near the ancient theatre [Epidaurus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epidaurus "Epidaurus"). These buildings have inwardly sloping walls, but bear no other resemblance to Egyptian pyramids. They had large central rooms (unlike Egyptian pyramids) and the Hellenikon structure is rectangular rather than square, 12.5 by 14 metres (41 by 46 ft) which means that the sides could not have met at a point.[[23]](#cite_note-23) The stone used to build these structures was limestone quarried locally and was cut to fit, not into freestanding blocks like the Great Pyramid of Giza.[[24]](#cite_note-24)

These structures were dated from pot shards excavated from the floor and grounds. The latest estimates are around the 5th and 4th centuries. Normally this technique is used for dating [pottery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pottery "Pottery"), but researchers used it to try to date stone flakes from the structure walls. This launched debate about whether or not these structures are actually older than [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt "Egypt"), part of the [Black Athena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Athena "Black Athena") controversy.[[25]](#cite_note-25)

[Lefkowitz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mary_Lefkowitz "Mary Lefkowitz") criticised this research, suggesting that some of the research was done not to determine the reliability of the dating method, as was suggested, but to back up a claim and to make points about pyramids and Greek civilization. She claimed that not only were the results imprecise, but that other structures mentioned in the research are not in fact pyramids, e.g. a tomb alleged to be the tomb of [Amphion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amphion "Amphion") and Zethus near [Thebes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thebes,_Greece "Thebes, Greece"), a structure at Stylidha ([Thessaly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thessaly "Thessaly")) which is a long wall, etc. She pushed the possibility that the stones that were dated might have been recycled from earlier constructions. She claimed that earlier research from the 1930s, confirmed in the 1980s by Fracchia, was ignored.[[26]](#cite_note-26)

Liritzis responded that Lefkowitz failed to understand and misinterpreted the methodology.[[27]](#cite_note-27)

#### Poland

Kuyavian Pyramids, known as Kuyavian Mounds, Graves of Giants or Polish Pyramids – enormous tombs, megalithic structures in [Kuyavia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuyavia "Kuyavia"), [Poland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poland "Poland").

#### Spain

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Pyramide_G%C3%BCimar.jpg/250px-Pyramide_G%C3%BCimar.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pyramide_G%C3%BCimar.jpg)

[Pyramids of Güímar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramids_of_G%C3%BC%C3%ADmar "Pyramids of Güímar"), [Tenerife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenerife "Tenerife"), Spain

The [Pyramids of Güímar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramids_of_G%C3%BC%C3%ADmar "Pyramids of Güímar") refer to six rectangular pyramid-shaped, terraced structures, built from [lava](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lava "Lava") without [mortar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mortar_\(masonry\) "Mortar (masonry)"). They are located in the district of Chacona, part of the town of [Güímar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BC%C3%ADmar "Güímar") on the island of [Tenerife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenerife "Tenerife") in the [Canary Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canary_Islands "Canary Islands"). The structures were dated to the 19th century and their function explained as a byproduct of contemporary [agricultural](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agricultural "Agricultural") techniques.

[Autochthonous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indigenous_peoples "Indigenous peoples") [Guanche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guanches "Guanches") traditions as well as surviving images indicate that similar structures (also known as, "Morras", "Majanos", "Molleros", or "Paredones") were built in many locations on the island.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_] However, over time they were dismantled and used as building material. [Güímar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BC%C3%ADmar "Güímar") hostred nine pyramids, only six of which survive.

#### Roman Empire

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Pyramid_of_cestius.jpg/250px-Pyramid_of_cestius.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pyramid_of_cestius.jpg)

[Pyramid of Cestius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_Cestius "Pyramid of Cestius") in Rome, Italy

The 27-metre-high [Pyramid of Cestius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_Cestius "Pyramid of Cestius") was built by the end of the 1st century BC and survives close to the [Porta San Paolo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porta_San_Paolo "Porta San Paolo"). Another, named _[Meta Romuli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meta_Romuli "Meta Romuli")_, stood in the _Ager Vaticanus_ (today's [Borgo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borgo_\(rione_of_Rome\) "Borgo (rione of Rome)")), but was destroyed at the end of the 15th century.[[28]](#cite_note-28)

#### Medieval Europe

Pyramids were occasionally used in [Christian architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_architecture "Church architecture") during the [feudal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feudalism "Feudalism") era, e.g. as the tower of [Oviedo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oviedo "Oviedo")'s Gothic [Cathedral of San Salvador](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathedral_of_San_Salvador_\(Oviedo\) "Cathedral of San Salvador (Oviedo)").

### Americas

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/PyramidOfTheMoonTeotihuacan.jpg/250px-PyramidOfTheMoonTeotihuacan.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:PyramidOfTheMoonTeotihuacan.jpg)

[Pyramid of the Moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_the_Moon "Pyramid of the Moon"), [Teotihuacan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teotihuacan "Teotihuacan"), built between 100 and 450 AD

#### Peru

[Andean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andean_civilizations "Andean civilizations") cultures used pyramids in various architectural structures such as the ones in [Caral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caral "Caral"), [Túcume](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%C3%BAcume "Túcume") and [Chavín de Huantar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chav%C3%ADn_de_Huantar "Chavín de Huantar"), constructed around the same time as early Egyptian pyramids.

#### Mesoamerica

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Chichen_Itza_3.jpg/250px-Chichen_Itza_3.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Chichen_Itza_3.jpg)

[El Castillo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Castillo,_Chichen_Itza "El Castillo, Chichen Itza") at [Chichen Itza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chichen_Itza "Chichen Itza")

Several [Mesoamerican](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerica "Mesoamerica") cultures built pyramid-shaped structures. [Mesoamerican pyramids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerican_pyramids "Mesoamerican pyramids") were usually stepped, with temples on top, more similar to the Mesopotamian ziggurat than the Egyptian pyramid.

The largest by volume is the [Great Pyramid of Cholula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Cholula "Great Pyramid of Cholula"), in the Mexican state of [Puebla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puebla "Puebla"). Constructed from the 3rd century BC to the 9th century AD, this pyramid is the world's largest monument, and is still not fully excavated. The third largest pyramid in the world, the [Pyramid of the Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_the_Sun "Pyramid of the Sun"), at [Teotihuacan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teotihuacan "Teotihuacan"), is also located in [Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico "Mexico"). An unusual pyramid with a circular plan survives at the site of [Cuicuilco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuicuilco "Cuicuilco"), now inside [Mexico City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico_City "Mexico City") and mostly covered with lava from an eruption of the [Xitle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xitle "Xitle") Volcano in the 1st century BC. Several circular stepped pyramids called [Guachimontones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guachimontones "Guachimontones") survive in Teuchitlán, [Jalisco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jalisco "Jalisco").

Pyramids in Mexico were often used for [human sacrifice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_sacrifice "Human sacrifice"). [Harner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Harner "Michael Harner") stated that for the dedication of the [Great Pyramid of Tenochtitlan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Tenochtitlan "Great Pyramid of Tenochtitlan") in 1487, "one source states 20,000, another 72,344, and several give 80,400" as the number of humans sacrificed.[[29]](#cite_note-29)

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Monk_Mound_-_Flickr_-_GregTheBusker.jpg/250px-Monk_Mound_-_Flickr_-_GregTheBusker.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Monk_Mound_-_Flickr_-_GregTheBusker.jpg)

[Monks Mound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monks_Mound "Monks Mound"), [Cahokia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cahokia "Cahokia")

Many pre-Columbian [Native American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States "Native Americans in the United States") societies of ancient North America built large pyramidal [earth structures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_structure "Earth structure") known as [platform mounds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Platform_mound "Platform mound"). Among the largest and best-known of these structures is [Monks Mound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monks_Mound "Monks Mound") at the site of [Cahokia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cahokia "Cahokia") in what became [Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illinois "Illinois"), completed around 1100 AD. It has a base larger than that of the Great Pyramid. Many mounds underwent repeated episodes of expansion. They are believed to have played a central role in the mound-building peoples' religious life. Documented uses include semi-public [chief](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribal_chief "Tribal chief")'s house platforms, public [temple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temple "Temple") platforms, [mortuary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mortuary "Mortuary") platforms, [charnel house](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charnel_house "Charnel house") platforms, [earth lodge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth_lodge "Earth lodge")/town house platforms, residence platforms, square ground and rotunda platforms, and dance platforms.[[30]](#cite_note-PLATFORM-30)[[31]](#cite_note-HANDBOOK-31)[[32]](#cite_note-FLORIDAANTHRO-32) Cultures that built substructure mounds include the [Troyville culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troyville_culture "Troyville culture"), [Coles Creek culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coles_Creek_culture "Coles Creek culture"), [Plaquemine culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plaquemine_culture "Plaquemine culture") and [Mississippian cultures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippian_culture "Mississippian culture").

#### India

Numerous giant, [granite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Granite "Granite"), temple pyramids were built in [South India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_India "South India") during the [Chola Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chola_Empire "Chola Empire"), many of which remain in use. Examples include [Brihadisvara Temple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brihadisvara_Temple,_Thanjavur "Brihadisvara Temple, Thanjavur") at [Thanjavur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thanjavur "Thanjavur"), [Brihadisvara Temple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brihadisvara_Temple,_Gangaikonda_Cholapuram "Brihadisvara Temple, Gangaikonda Cholapuram") at [Gangaikonda Cholapuram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gangaikonda_Cholapuram "Gangaikonda Cholapuram"), and the [Airavatesvara Temple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Airavatesvara_Temple "Airavatesvara Temple") at [Darasuram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Darasuram "Darasuram"). However, the largest temple (area) is the [Ranganathaswamy Temple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Ranganathaswamy_Temple_\(Srirangam\) "Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple (Srirangam)") in [Srirangam, Tamil Nadu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Srirangam "Srirangam"). The Thanjavur temple was built by Raja Raja Chola in the 11th century. The [Brihadisvara Temple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brihadisvara_Temple,_Thanjavur "Brihadisvara Temple, Thanjavur") was declared a [World Heritage Site](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Heritage_Site "World Heritage Site") by [UNESCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO") in 1987; the Temple of Gangaikondacholapuram and the Airavatesvara Temple at Darasuram were added in 2004.[[33]](#cite_note-33)

#### Indonesia

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Borobudur-Nothwest-view.jpg/250px-Borobudur-Nothwest-view.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Borobudur-Nothwest-view.jpg)

[Borobudur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borobudur "Borobudur"), [Central Java](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Java "Central Java"), Indonesia

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Candi_Sukuh_2007.JPG/250px-Candi_Sukuh_2007.JPG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Candi_Sukuh_2007.JPG)

[Candi Sukuh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Candi_Sukuh "Candi Sukuh") in [Java](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Java "Java"), Indonesia

[Austronesian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_people "Austronesian people") [megalithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megalithic "Megalithic") culture in [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia") featured earth and stone [step pyramid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Step_pyramid "Step pyramid") structures called _punden berundak._ These were discovered in Pangguyangan near Cisolok[[34]](#cite_note-34) and in Cipari near Kuningan.[[35]](#cite_note-35) The stone pyramids were based on beliefs that mountains and high places were the abode for the spirit of the [ancestors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancestors "Ancestors").[[36]](#cite_note-36)

The step pyramid is the basic design of the 8th century [Borobudur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borobudur "Borobudur") Buddhist monument in [Central Java](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Java "Central Java").[[37]](#cite_note-37) However later Java temples were influenced by Indian [Hindu architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindu_architecture "Hindu architecture"), as exemplified by the spires of [Prambanan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prambanan "Prambanan") temple. In the 15th century, during late [Majapahit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Majapahit "Majapahit") period, Java saw the revival of indigenous Austronesian elements as displayed by [Sukuh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Candi_Sukuh "Candi Sukuh") temple that somewhat resemble Mesoamerican pyramids, and also stepped pyramids of Mount Penanggungan.[[38]](#cite_note-38)

### Modern mausoleums

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/MetCemBrunswigPyramid1.jpg/250px-MetCemBrunswigPyramid1.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MetCemBrunswigPyramid1.jpg)

Metairie Cemetery, [New Orleans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Orleans "New Orleans")

With the [Egyptian Revival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Revival "Egyptian Revival") movement in the nineteenth and early twentieth century, pyramids became more common in funerary architecture. The tomb of [Quintino Sella](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quintino_Sella "Quintino Sella"), outside the monumental cemetery of [Oropa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oropa "Oropa"), is pyramid-shaped.[[44]](#cite_note-44) This style was popular with [tycoons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tycoon "Tycoon") in the US. The [Schoenhofen Pyramid Mausoleum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schoenhofen_Pyramid_Mausoleum "Schoenhofen Pyramid Mausoleum") (1889) in [Chicago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago "Chicago") and [Hunt's Tomb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunt%27s_Tomb "Hunt's Tomb") (1930) in [Phoenix, Arizona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona "Phoenix, Arizona") are notable examples. Some people build pyramid tombs for themselves. [Nicolas Cage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolas_Cage "Nicolas Cage") bought a pyramid tomb for himself in a famed [New Orleans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Orleans "New Orleans") graveyard.[[45]](#cite_note-45)
